# Collection Playbook

Pick the stage, then write in that stage's voice. Days are guidance; account state (payment, dispute, promise) always wins over raw aging.

## Decision order

1. Payment received → acknowledgment only, no collection language.
2. Payment credibly in transit → request traceable remittance details; never press for a duplicate payment.
3. Active dispute, deduction, PO, tax, receiving, or service issue → draft toward resolving that issue; don't demand the disputed amount as though undisputed.
4. Partial payment or unapplied cash → reconcile the components; ask about the specific residual.
5. Missed payment commitment → address the broken promise, not the aging bucket.
6. Otherwise → choose by days overdue below.

## Stages

| Stage | Trigger | Ask | Voice |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pre-due | 3–7 days before due | Confirm invoice is approved and scheduled | Service-oriented; offer to help keep it on schedule |
| Due today | Due date | Confirm payment is being released | Direct, neutral |
| Early overdue | 1–7 days past due | Status plus a specific payment date | Courteous but definite |
| Active follow-up | 8–30 days past due | A committed payment date | Firm, concise; reference prior contact if supplied |
| Escalated | 31–60 days past due | Immediate owner and date | Senior, commercially serious; no unsupported threats |
| Mature | 60+ days past due (even with no history supplied) | Remittance details if paid, a firm payment date if not, with a response deadline | Pointed, controlled; state the aging plainly |
| Missed promise | Commitment date passed | What changed, and a revised specific date | Reference the exact promise, neutrally |
| Dispute/hold | Any unresolved blocker | Resolve the named blocker; agree the next checkpoint | Cooperative, precise; acknowledge the issue by name |

At the Mature stage: no open-ended "could you let me know" as the main ask, no "happy to help" close. Include a response deadline of two business days from today — this is a sender-selected response deadline, so also flag it after the draft as "Note: response deadline of <date> is my suggestion — adjust to your cadence." Do not present it as a contractual due date.

## Subject lines

Specific and low-drama: `Invoice INV-1048 — payment status`, `Payment date confirmation — INV-1048`, `Action required: INV-1048, 63 days past due`, `Resolving PO discrepancy for INV-1048`. No all-caps, no vague "Following up".

## Greetings and sign-offs (no placeholders)

- Contact name known → "Hi Dana," / "Dear Ms. Whitfield,"
- No name → "Hi team," or "Dear Accounts Payable team,"
- Sign with the sender's name or company from the material; if none, "Best regards,\nAccounts Receivable"

## Message shape

Context (which invoice, why writing) → fact (amount, due date, days overdue — correctly labeled) → relevant history only if supplied → one specific ask → invitation to flag a blocker or send remittance → brief close.

## Tone by example

**Pre-due:** "I'm checking that invoice INV-1048 is approved and scheduled for payment ahead of its July 17 due date. Let me know if your team needs anything from us to keep it on schedule."

**Early overdue:** "Invoice INV-1048 for $18,750 was due July 8. Could you confirm its current status and expected payment date? If payment has already been released, please share the remittance details so we can apply it correctly."

**Active follow-up:** "I'm following up on invoice INV-1048, now 22 days past due. We haven't received payment confirmation since our July 18 note. Please confirm the payment date, or let us know what's preventing release."

**Missed promise:** "You confirmed invoice INV-1048 would be in the July 24 payment run, but we haven't received payment or remittance advice. Please confirm whether it was released and, if not, the revised payment date."

**Mature (60+, payment status unverified):** "Invoice IN-021 for $750, due May 12, is now 63 days past due. Please confirm its payment status by July 17. If payment has been released, send the remittance details so we can trace and apply it. If it remains unpaid, provide a confirmed payment date and let us know of any issue preventing release."

## Gold-standard example (full email)

Input: invoice INV-2214, $12,400, due May 5; today July 15; no contact name; no payment history supplied.

```
Subject: Action required: INV-2214, 71 days past due

Dear Accounts Payable team,

Invoice INV-2214 for $12,400.00, due May 5, is now 71 days past due. We need to resolve this promptly.

If payment has been released, please send the remittance details so we can trace and apply it. If it remains unpaid, please reply by July 17 with a confirmed payment date and let us know of any issue that has been holding it up.

Best regards,
Accounts Receivable
```

## Multiple invoices

List them compactly, state a total only when the components are confirmed and consistent, make one account-level ask, then name invoice-specific blockers.
